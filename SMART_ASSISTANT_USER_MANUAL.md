## 1. Overview

### What is Smart Assistant?

Smart Assistant is a self-service attendance marking system that allows external visitors to quickly check-in for meetings **after logging in with valid credentials**. It's designed for reception desks, meeting rooms, or self-service kiosks.

### Key Benefits:
- ✅ **Secure Login** - Authenticated access for authorized users
- ✅ **Two Search Methods** - Find meetings by Meeting ID or Reference Value
- ✅ **Real-time Attendance** - Instant confirmation
- ✅ **Visitor Management** - Add new visitors on the fly
- ✅ **User-Friendly** - Simple, intuitive interface
- ✅ **Secure** - Protected data with validation

---

## 2. Getting Started

### How to Access Smart Assistant

1. **Login to the system**
   - Open your web browser
   - Navigate to the login page
   - Enter your username and password
   - Click "Login" to access the system

2. **Navigate to Smart Assistant**
   - After logging in, go to the Smart Assistant page (provided by your administrator)
   - You'll see the Smart Assistant welcome screen
   - Now you can enter your Meeting ID or Reference Value to search for meetings

### System Requirements

- **Web Browser**: Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari (latest versions recommended)
- **Internet Connection**: Required for real-time updates
- **Device**: Desktop computer, tablet, or mobile phone

## 3. Features

### 3.1 Search Options

Smart Assistant offers two ways to find your meeting:

#### **Option 1: Search by Meeting ID** 🔢
- Use the 6-character Meeting ID provided in your invitation email
- Example: `ABC123`
- **Best for**: Quick check-in when you have the Meeting ID

#### **Option 2: Search by Reference Value** 👤
- Use your reference number (NIC, Passport, or Employee ID)
- Example: Your passport number or ID number
- **Best for**: Finding meetings when you don't have the Meeting ID

### 3.2 Meeting Details View

Once you find your meeting, you'll see:
- ✅ Meeting title and description
- ✅ Date and time
- ✅ Location/Place
- ✅ Meeting organizer name
- ✅ List of all external visitors
- ✅ Your attendance status

### 3.3 Attendance Marking

- **Today's Meetings**: You can mark attendance for meetings scheduled today
- **Future/Past Meetings**: View-only mode (cannot mark attendance)
- **Confirmation**: Instant confirmation when attendance is marked

### 3.4 Member Management

- **Add Existing Members**: Search and add members from the database
- **Create New Members**: Add new visitors on the spot
- **Real-time Updates**: Changes reflect immediately

## 4. Step-by-Step Guides

### Guide 1: Finding and Marking Attendance Using Meeting ID

#### Step 1: Access Smart Assistant
1. Open the Smart Assistant page in your browser
2. You'll see the search screen with two tabs: "Meeting ID" and "Reference Value"

#### Step 2: Enter Meeting ID
1. Make sure **"Meeting ID"** tab is selected (it's selected by default)
2. Enter your **6-character Meeting ID** in the input field
   - Example: `ABC123`
   - The system automatically converts it to uppercase
   - You can enter it in any case (lowercase, uppercase, or mixed)
3. Click the **"Find Meeting"** button or press **Enter**

#### Step 3: View Meeting Details
1. If your Meeting ID is valid, you'll see the meeting details page
2. Review the meeting information:
   - Meeting title
   - Date and time
   - Location
   - Organizer name

#### Step 4: Select Your Name
1. Scroll down to see the **"Select Your Name to Mark Attendance"** section
2. Find your name in the visitor list
3. **Click on your name card** to select it

#### Step 5: Confirm Attendance
1. Review your information on the confirmation page
2. Verify:
   - Meeting details are correct
   - Your personal information is correct
3. Click the **"Confirm Attendance"** button

#### Step 6: Success!
1. You'll see a success message: **"Attendance Confirmed!"**
2. Your check-in time is recorded automatically
3. You can:
   - Mark attendance for another visitor
   - Go back to login page

---

### Guide 2: Finding and Marking Attendance Using Reference Value

#### Step 1: Switch to Reference Search
1. On the Smart Assistant search screen
2. Click the **"Reference Value"** tab

#### Step 2: Enter Your Reference Value
1. Enter your reference number in the input field:
   - **NIC Number**: Your national ID number
   - **Passport Number**: Your passport number
   - **Employee ID**: Your employee identification number
2. Click **"Find Meeting"** or press **Enter**

#### Step 3: Find Today's Meeting
1. The system will search for all meetings associated with your reference value
2. It will automatically filter for **today's meetings only**
3. If found, you'll see the meeting details

#### Step 4: Select Your Name
1. Find your name in the visitor list
2. Click on your name card

#### Step 5: Confirm Attendance
1. Review and confirm your information
2. Click **"Confirm Attendance"**

#### Step 6: Success!
- You'll see the confirmation message
- Your attendance is recorded

---

### Guide 3: What If My Meeting Is Not Today?

If you search for a meeting that's not scheduled for today:

#### **Future Meetings** (Scheduled for later dates)
- You can **view** the meeting details
- You can **see** the visitor list
- You **cannot** mark attendance
- A message will show: *"This meeting is not scheduled for today. Attendance marking is only available for today's bookings."*

#### **Past Meetings** (Already completed)
- You can **view** the meeting details
- You can **see** the visitor list
- You **cannot** mark attendance
- A message will show: *"This meeting was scheduled for a past date."*

**Note**: Attendance marking is **only available for meetings scheduled for today**.

## 5. Adding Members to Meetings

Smart Assistant allows you to add new visitors to a meeting on the spot. This is useful when:
- A visitor arrives who wasn't originally in the meeting list
- You need to register a walk-in visitor
- Adding additional participants

### Guide 4: Adding an Existing Member to a Meeting

#### Step 1: Open Add Member Dialog
1. After finding your meeting, scroll to the visitor list
2. Click the **"Add Member"** button (top right of the visitor list)

#### Step 2: Search for Existing Member
1. In the dialog, use the **"Search Existing Members by Reference"** section
2. Type at least 2 characters to search
3. You can search by:
   - Reference value (NIC, Passport, Employee ID)
   - Name
   - Email
   - Phone number
   - Company name

#### Step 3: Select Member
1. Review the search results
2. Click on the member you want to add
3. The member will be automatically added to the meeting

#### Step 4: Confirmation
- A success message will appear: *"Added [Name] to the meeting"*
- The member appears in the visitor list immediately

---

### Guide 5: Creating and Adding a New Member

#### Step 1: Open Add Member Dialog
1. Click the **"Add Member"** button from the meeting details page

#### Step 2: Create New Member
1. In the dialog, scroll down to **"Create New Member"** section
2. Click the **"Create New"** button

#### Step 3: Fill in Member Information

**Required Fields** (marked with *):
- **Full Name***: Enter the visitor's full name
- **Email***: Enter a valid email address
- **Phone***: Enter phone number
- **Reference Type***: Select from dropdown:
  - NIC
  - Passport
  - Employee ID
- **Reference Value***: Enter the reference number

**Optional Fields**:
- **Company Name**: Enter company name (if applicable)
- **Designation**: Enter job title (if applicable)

#### Step 4: Submit
1. Review all information
2. Click **"Create & Add to Meeting"**
3. The system will:
   - Create a new member record (if doesn't exist)
   - Add them to the current meeting
   - Show them in the visitor list

#### Step 5: Confirmation
- Success message: *"Added [Name] to the meeting"*
- The new member can now mark their attendance

## 6. Troubleshooting

### Problem: "Meeting ID not found"

**Possible Causes**:
- ❌ Incorrect Meeting ID entered
- ❌ Meeting ID has expired
- ❌ Meeting was cancelled or deleted

**Solutions**:
1. ✅ Double-check the Meeting ID from your invitation email
2. ✅ Make sure there are no spaces or extra characters
3. ✅ Try the "Reference Value" search method instead
4. ✅ Contact the meeting organizer for assistance

---

### Problem: "No meetings found for this reference"

**Possible Causes**:
- ❌ Reference value doesn't match any meetings
- ❌ No meetings scheduled for today with this reference
- ❌ Reference value was entered incorrectly

**Solutions**:
1. ✅ Verify your reference number (NIC, Passport, or Employee ID)
2. ✅ Check if you have any meetings scheduled for today
3. ✅ Try using the Meeting ID search method
4. ✅ Contact reception or the meeting organizer

---

### Problem: "Meetings found but they are scheduled for future dates"

**Explanation**:
- This is not an error - it's a feature
- Attendance marking is only available for today's meetings

**What You Can Do**:
- ✅ View the meeting details (view-only mode)
- ✅ See the visitor list
- ✅ Wait until the meeting date to mark attendance
- ✅ Contact the organizer if you need to mark attendance early

---

### Problem: "Attendance has already been marked for this visitor"

**Explanation**:
- You've already marked your attendance for this meeting
- The system prevents duplicate attendance entries

**What You'll See**:
- ✅ A green checkmark (✓) next to your name
- ✅ "Confirmed" status
- ✅ Your name card highlighted in green

**Note**: You cannot mark attendance twice for the same meeting.

---

### Problem: Can't find my name in the visitor list

**Possible Causes**:
- ❌ Your name wasn't added to the meeting
- ❌ Different name spelling or format

**Solutions**:
1. ✅ Ask the meeting organizer to add you to the meeting
2. ✅ Use "Add Member" to add yourself (if you have access)
3. ✅ Check if your name appears with a different spelling
4. ✅ Contact reception for assistance

---

### Problem: Page not loading or slow response

**Solutions**:
1. ✅ Check your internet connection
2. ✅ Refresh the page (F5 or Ctrl+R)
3. ✅ Clear browser cache
4. ✅ Try a different browser
5. ✅ Check if the system is under maintenance (contact IT support)

## 7. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: Do I need to create an account to use Smart Assistant?

**Answer**: Yes! Smart Assistant requires a valid account and login credentials. You must log in with your username and password first before you can search for meetings and mark attendance.

---

### Q2: What is a Meeting ID?

**Answer**: A Meeting ID is a unique 6-character code assigned to each meeting. You'll receive it in your meeting invitation email. Example: `ABC123`.

---

### Q3: Can I mark attendance for meetings that are tomorrow or next week?

**Answer**: No. Attendance marking is only available for meetings scheduled for **today**. You can view future meetings but cannot mark attendance until the meeting date.

---

### Q4: Can I mark attendance multiple times?

**Answer**: No. Once you mark attendance for a meeting, you cannot mark it again. The system will show you as "Confirmed" with a green checkmark.

---

### Q5: What if I don't have my Meeting ID?

**Answer**: You can use the **"Reference Value"** search method instead. Enter your NIC, Passport, or Employee ID number to find your meeting.

---

### Q6: Can I add myself to a meeting if I'm not on the list?

**Answer**: If you're using Smart Assistant at a reception desk or kiosk, you may be able to add yourself using the "Add Member" feature. Otherwise, contact the meeting organizer to add you.

---

### Q7: What happens if I enter the wrong Meeting ID?

**Answer**: The system will show an error message: *"Meeting ID not found. Please check your Meeting ID and try again."* Simply re-enter the correct Meeting ID.

---

### Q8: Is my information secure?

**Answer**: Yes! Smart Assistant uses secure connections and follows data protection best practices. Your information is only used for attendance marking and meeting management.

---

### Q9: Can I see my attendance history?

**Answer**: Smart Assistant is designed for check-in only. To view your attendance history, contact the meeting organizer or use the main system (if you have an account).

---

### Q10: What if I arrive late to the meeting?

**Answer**: You can still mark attendance even if you arrive late. The system records your actual check-in time automatically.

---

### Q11: Can I mark attendance for someone else?

**Answer**: No. Each person must mark their own attendance by selecting their name from the visitor list. This ensures accurate attendance records.

---

### Q12: What browsers are supported?

**Answer**: Smart Assistant works best with:
- ✅ Google Chrome (recommended)
- ✅ Mozilla Firefox
- ✅ Microsoft Edge
- ✅ Safari

---

### Q13: Does it work on mobile phones?

**Answer**: Yes! Smart Assistant is responsive and works on mobile phones, tablets, and desktop computers.

---

### Q14: What if the system says "No bookings found"?

**Answer**: This usually means:
- The meeting hasn't been created yet
- The meeting was cancelled
- There's a technical issue

Contact the meeting organizer or reception for assistance.

## 8. Best Practices

### For Visitors:

1. **Save Your Meeting ID**
   - Keep your Meeting ID handy (save the invitation email)
   - Take a screenshot for quick reference

2. **Arrive Early**
   - Mark your attendance a few minutes before the meeting starts
   - This helps organizers know who has arrived

3. **Double-Check Information**
   - Verify meeting details before confirming attendance
   - Make sure you're marking attendance for the correct meeting

4. **Use Reference Value as Backup**
   - If you forget your Meeting ID, use your reference number
   - Always have your NIC/Passport number available

5. **Contact Organizer if Issues**
   - If you can't find your meeting, contact the organizer
   - They can add you to the meeting or provide the correct Meeting ID

### For Reception Staff:

1. **Guide Visitors**
   - Help visitors navigate the Smart Assistant interface
   - Explain the search methods clearly

2. **Add Walk-ins Quickly**
   - Use the "Add Member" feature for visitors not on the list
   - Create new member records when needed

3. **Verify Today's Meetings**
   - Confirm the meeting is scheduled for today
   - Check if attendance marking is available

4. **Troubleshoot Issues**
   - Know the common problems and solutions
   - Contact IT support for technical issues

5. **Maintain Professionalism**
   - Provide clear instructions
   - Be patient and helpful
